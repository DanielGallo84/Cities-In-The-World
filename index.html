NEW YORK
New York is the most ethnically diverse, religiously varied, commercially driven, famously congested, and, in the eyes of many, the most attractive urban centre in the country.

PARIS
What Is Paris Famous For? | Celebrity Cruises Paris is known for its gorgeous, imposing monuments. These iconic structures, often an exemplar of a particular era in architecture, are one of the city's instantly recognizable elements. The preeminent of Paris's landmarks is the Eiffel Tower.

TOKYO
Tokyo, the capital of Japan, is vibrant as a city that leads Japan and the world. One of the attractions of Tokyo is that people from various regions and countries gather and interact with each other, and there is a tendency for many cultures to merge and to create new things.

ROME
Rome, the “Eternal City,” brims with ancient history, from the Colosseum to the port of Ostia Antica to majestic Vatican City and the Sistine Chapel. Because of its history, art, architecture, and beauty – and perhaps its gelato and pasta! – Rome is one of our most popular cities.

ISTANBUL
Istanbul, Turkey's vibrant metropolis, straddles two continents, blending a rich tapestry of cultures and histories. It is a city where East truly meets West, offering a unique fusion of tradition and modernity. With its stunning architecture, Istanbul showcases the remnants of ancient civilizations.

HONG KONG
Hong Kong has one of the world's most thriving economies and is a hub for international trade and investment. A cosmopolitan city, Hong Kong weaves Western and Asian influence into a world-class center of business, culture, and trade.

At Rochelle Canteen, they serve a daily changing menu – perhaps grilled onglet with cavolo nero or smoked with fish. It’s unfussy and honest cooking, served in a setting that feels more country house than East End.
